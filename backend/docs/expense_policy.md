# Expense Policy

## Purpose

This expense policy defines how employees may incur, submit, and receive reimbursement for business-related expenses. The goal is to ensure expenses are reasonable, compliant, and processed efficiently while enabling employees to do their jobs effectively.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to all full-time, part-time, and contract employees who incur expenses on behalf of the company.

---

## General Principles

- Expenses must be **necessary, reasonable, and directly related to business activities**
- Employees should exercise **good judgment** and spend company money as if it were their own
- Reimbursement is subject to manager approval and finance review
- Expenses that are excessive, personal, or undocumented may be denied

---

## Reimbursable Expenses

### Travel

The following travel-related expenses are eligible for reimbursement when incurred for approved business purposes:

- Airfare (economy class or equivalent)
- Lodging at reasonably priced hotels
- Ground transportation (rideshare, taxi, public transit, rental car)
- Mileage for personal vehicle use at the IRS standard rate
- Baggage fees for business travel

> Employees should book travel using preferred vendors when available.

---

### Meals

- Meals during business travel
- Meals with clients or candidates
- Team meals during approved offsites or working sessions

**Guidelines:**
- Alcohol is reimbursable only when associated with a business meal and in moderation
- Lavish or excessive meals may be denied
- Itemized receipts are required

---

### Office & Equipment

- Office supplies required for work
- Ergonomic equipment (e.g., chair, keyboard, monitor) with prior approval
- Software or tools not otherwise provided by the company

---

### Communication & Internet

- Mobile phone plans (business portion only, if applicable)
- Home internet reimbursement (partial reimbursement with approval)

---

### Professional Development

- Conferences, workshops, and training courses
- Certification fees related to job responsibilities
- Professional memberships and subscriptions

> Pre-approval is required for most professional development expenses.

---

## Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following are generally not reimbursable:

- Personal expenses not related to work
- First-class or luxury travel upgrades
- Entertainment unrelated to business
- Fines, penalties, or parking tickets
- Expenses for family members or guests
- Late fees or interest charges

---

## Receipts & Documentation

- Receipts are required for all expenses over **$25**
- Receipts must be itemized and clearly legible
- Credit card statements alone are not sufficient documentation
- Missing receipts may require a written explanation and are subject to approval

---

## Submission & Approval Process

1. Submit expenses through the company’s expense system within **30 days** of incurring the expense
2. Include all required receipts and notes
3. Manager approval is required for reimbursement
4. Finance will review for compliance and process payment

---

## Reimbursement Timing

- Approved expenses are reimbursed via payroll or direct deposit
- Reimbursements are typically processed within **2–3 payroll cycles**

---

## Policy Exceptions

- Any exception to this policy must be approved in advance by Finance
- Repeated violations may result in denied reimbursements or disciplinary action

---

## Questions

If you have questions about whether an expense is reimbursable, contact **Finance** before incurring the cost.
